Australian Financial Markets Overview

Australia has one of the largest financial markets in the Asia-Pacific region. The Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) is the primary stock exchange, located in Sydney. It lists over 2,000 companies and has a market capitalization of approximately $2.5 trillion AUD.

The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) is the central bank responsible for monetary policy. The RBA sets the official cash rate, which influences interest rates across the economy. In recent years, the RBA has adjusted rates to manage inflation and economic growth.

Australian Superannuation is a compulsory retirement savings system. Employers are required to contribute a percentage of employee salaries into superannuation funds. Australia's superannuation system manages over $3.5 trillion AUD in assets, making it one of the largest pension systems in the world.

The four major banks in Australia are Commonwealth Bank, Westpac, ANZ, and NAB. These banks dominate the retail banking sector and are regulated by the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA).

Australian government bonds are considered one of the safest investments. The yield on 10-year Australian government bonds serves as a benchmark for other interest rates in the economy.

Inflation in Australia is measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The RBA targets an inflation rate of 2-3% per year to maintain price stability and economic growth.
